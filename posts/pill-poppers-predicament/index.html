Interview Question - Pill Popper's Predicament


Overview #

You have 10 jars, each containing 100 pills. Pills normally weigh 1g, however one of the jars contains "fake" pills weighing only 0.9g. Given a set of scales accurate to 1g, and the opportunity to weigh only 1 jar, suggest how the jar of fake pills could be identified.

Solution #

Click to reveal

Empty all jars into piles and label numerically. Take one of the now empty jars and fill with a selection of pills from each pile. Multiples of 10 must be used to satisfy the 1g accuracy limitation of the scales. Weighing the resulting jar will produce a shortfall between 1g and 10g which can be used to identify the fake jar, as shown in the table below.

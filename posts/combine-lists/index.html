Interview Question - Combining Two Lists


Overview #

You are presented with two lists of integer numbers with the following properties:

  1. The numbers are not in sequence
  2. The numbers in each list are unique
  3. Duplicates may exist between the two lists
     

Example lists #

A: 15321, 7, 3, 52, 750, 37, 1, 575, 38098, 0, ...
B: 17, 357, 7, 2, 750, 1, 9, 12, 32, 689, 555, ...

 

Question #

Describe an algorithm to combine the two lists such that:

  1. All unique numbers from both lists are contained within the result
  2. The result contains only 1 instance of any duplicates

Where appropriate we ask you to take into account that:

  • The lists may be very large
  • The algorithm may be executing in a realtime context
  • The hardware executing the code may be limited

You are encouraged to think out loud and show working - we are interested in your process as much as the result.
